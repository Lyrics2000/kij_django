Our History

AIC Kijabe Printing Press was established in the year 1915 as an arm of Africa Inland Church to provide reading materials for the propagation of the Gospel. The technology and low demand at the time dictated for itâ€™s humble beginning whereby at the beginning a stationary bicycle was peddled to provide the power needed to run the printing machine. However, as demand increased, a diesel engine was used to provide the power.

In response to increasing printing needs, Africa Inland Church in collaboration with Africa Inland Mission (AIM) found it necessary in 1953 to make the Printing Press a fully fledged department of the church. In 1971, A.I.M. handed over the running of the Press to the AIC Central Church Head Office.

Since then AIC Kijabe Printing Press has been under the A.I.C. Central Office who in their wisdom appoint a Board of Management to manage the Press. In 1984 Africa Inland Church Kijabe Printing Press was registered as a Company limited by guarantee and having no share capital.
